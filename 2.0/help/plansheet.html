This is a draft, a sketching board or workspace - use any word you like. It has several main characteristics:
1.Size - weight and height;  
2.Coordinate axes - the length of positive segments of the X and Y axes. The length of the positive segments should be less than the length of the corresponding size parameters.  
3.Scale.  

You can easily modify these parameters, go to Plot > Parameters in the main window (or Draft > Parameters in the Tiles editor or Patterns editor windows).

Buttons to zoom in/out are present on the toolbar. For more details, view the Scale article.

Scale depends on the draft's type: a tile and a pattern drafts are scaled in millimeters by default; a plot draft is scaled in meters by default. You can modify these metrics, and use centimeters and millimeters to scale the plot (view Program settings). A single step of the coordinate grid depends on the scale: for millimeters and centimeters scale it is 1 mm, for meter's scale - 10 cm.

Pointer coordinates are displayed at the top left corner of the draft. The pointer motion "is tied" to the junctions of the coordinate grid: it is more comfortable to draw correct rectangles and to control size. If you press and hold Ctrl the, the pointer will move randomly, not rounded off to the divisions of the coordinate grid. On practice it means that you may start drawing segments not only on the lattice junctions but also inside cells.

When you use large scale to draw big object, it is convenient to use mini-display to navigate on the draft.
